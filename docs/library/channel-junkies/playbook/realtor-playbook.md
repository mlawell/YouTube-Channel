# The Channel Junkies Realtor Playbook

**A synthesized, action-oriented guide to building a real-estate YouTube channel that generates buyer and seller leads.**

This playbook distills two Channel Junkies programs — *The Billion Dollar Channel Method* (the strategy, mindset, and story) and the *14 Day Sprint* (the practical how-to) — into one workflow a realtor can execute. Citations point back to the source: **Day N** = 14 Day Sprint video; **Ch N** = Billion Dollar Channel Method chapter. Verbatim transcripts with timestamps live in [`../transcripts`](../transcripts) and are indexed in the [volume README](../README.md).

> **The core idea:** YouTube is a *search engine*, not a social network. People search to relocate ("living in Houston", "pros and cons of moving to Portland"). If you answer that search with an authentic local video, the viewer arrives at your call already feeling like they know you (a "parasocial" relationship) and pre-sold. One channel built this way produced **327 closings / $174M in volume in under 3 years** (Ch 1).

---

## Part 1 — Strategy & Mindset (the "why")

1. **Treat your real estate business like a media company.** Sell homes, but build an audience that compounds (Intro, Ch 5).
2. **Be yourself, not a "professional."** Authenticity beats credentials. The "new to the area" angle ("I moved here a year ago") is often *more* trustworthy than claiming local-expert status, and your personality filters for your ideal clients (Ch 2, Ch 11).
3. **Search intent beats vanity metrics.** Views, subscribers, and social buzz don't pay you — *reach-outs* do. "Living in [city]" searches out-pull "market update [city]" by ~10x (Ch 6).
4. **Consistency beats quality, early.** Expect 6–12 months of low views before the algorithm rewards you. The breakout "pros and cons" video sat at 3–9 views for weeks, then grew to 57k (Ch 13).
5. **Quit what doesn't work, without guilt.** If a market produces no reach-outs after ~6–12 months, pause or kill it. Expensive/large metros take longer than small ones (Ch 14, Ch 18).
6. **You can't scale alone.** Plan from day one to delegate editing, admin, and eventually deal-closing so the business isn't trapped inside you (Ch 16, Ch 19).

## Part 2 — Channel Setup (14 Day Sprint, Days 1–3)

> The official templates for these steps (banner, About section, settings/auto-defaults, subscribe button, and the 10 titles + scripts) are in [`../resources`](../resources) — see the [volume README](../README.md#course-resources-14-day-sprint-downloads).

**Day 1 — Create a brand channel.**
- Use a **personal Gmail you'll own forever** — never a brokerage email (you lose the channel if you leave the brokerage) (Day 1, Day 3).
- Name it **"Living in [Largest Metro]"** (e.g., *Living in Houston Texas*), not a small suburb. Include "Team" even if you're solo, to position for future lead hand-off.
- Separate channels only when markets are 8+ hours apart.

**Day 2 — Brand visuals (don't perfect, ship).**
- Use Canva templates: banner (your face + city + "new videos every week") and a clean profile photo. Cap this at ~2 hours — ~70% of people who fail stall here (Day 2).

**Day 3 — Settings that unlock the algorithm.**
- Add an About description (phone, email, website) and a subscribe watermark.
- **Verify your phone** to unlock custom thumbnails, 15+ min videos, and monetization.
- **Channel keywords: 10–15 LOCAL terms only** ("living in Houston", "Katy homes", "pros and cons Houston"). **Never** "real estate / real estate agent" — national terms hurt the local algorithm.
- Category = **People & Blogs**; License = **Standard YouTube License** (protects your footage).
- Set **upload defaults** (paste the templated description once) so every future upload is pre-filled.

## Part 3 — The Content System (Days 4, 10 + Ch 6, 13)

**What to shoot, in priority order** (the content pyramid — Ch 13):
1. **Neighborhood vlogs** — what daily life actually looks like there.
2. **"Pros & Cons of Living in [City]"** — the proven starter (Day 4).
3. **Cost of Living** — keyword-driven, data-backed.
4. **Map / area-comparison videos** — highest retention; build authority (Day 10).
5. **Schools / lifestyle / commute** videos.
6. **Market updates** — lowest performing; do *not* prioritize.

**Title rule:** every title answers a search question.
- ❌ "Living in Portland"  →  ✅ "Pros & Cons of Living in Portland, Oregon (2025)" / "Cost of Living in Portland: What I Wish I Knew" (Ch 13).

**Video structure (Day 4, Ch 7):**
- **Hook (first ~3–30 sec):** name the viewer's question and tease the payoff. *"Thinking about moving to Portland? I did too a year ago — here are 4 neighborhoods you'll love and 4 you'll hate."* The first 30 seconds decide whether YouTube recommends it.
- **Branded intro (5–10 sec).**
- **Body (8–15 min):** alternate pros/cons, tell stories ("when I moved here…"), re-hook mid-video.
- **Call-to-action woven throughout**, value-first: *"We get relocations every day — shoot us a text."*
- **End screen (last 15 sec):** subscribe + next video.

**Map videos (Day 10)** — record Google Maps via Zoom/Loom screen-share, put yourself in the corner, narrate neighborhoods using Street View and personal stories, end with "call me — I know exactly where you'll want to live."

## Part 4 — Film, Edit, Publish (Days 5–7)

**Film (Day 5):**
- Outline bullet points on paper (not a full script — reading looks stiff). Practice the hook out loud.
- **Use the bullet-point storytelling method (Ch 20):** write 8–12 prompts of just 2–3 words each ("hunting", "Spirit Lake", "wedding speech") and tell one 2–5 minute story per bullet. That yields a flowing 15–20 min video with no script. Stories beat facts — "my dad lives here for the space next to the national forest" out-pulls "median price $473k." Don't sweat mispronouncing street/lake names; relocators don't know them either.
- **One continuous recording**; if you flub, pause and continue (don't stop/start — it creates an editing nightmare). Wave your hand as an editor "start here" cue.
- Tell stories, add personality/humor. First videos will be rough — that's normal and they still convert (Day 5).
- **Camera-shy? Use collaborative storytelling (Ch 20):** have a partner interview you to get you talking naturally, break it into 10–12 micro-segments (45–90 sec each), then edit those into one 12–18 min video.

**Edit (Day 6):**
- Use **iMovie** (Mac) or **Filmora** (Windows). **Do not use Adobe Premiere.**
- Store raw footage on a **1–2 TB external drive**; keep your computer 50–80% free.
- Cut with **Cmd/Ctrl+B**, add a title (don't cover your face), layer 5–15 sec B-roll, add CTA text. Export with the SEO filename ("Pros and Cons of Living in Houston Texas").

**Publish (Day 7):**
- Title auto-fills from the filename. Paste the templated, keyword-stuffed **description** (replace city/phone/email).
- Upload the **Canva thumbnail** (face + emotion + text).
- **Made for Kids = NO**; **Altered/AI content = NO** (both throttle reach).
- **Tags:** full title first, then local variations only. TubeBuddy optional.
- **Schedule consistently** (e.g., every Thursday evening or Saturday morning) to train audience + algorithm.

## Part 5 — Read the Analytics (Day 8, Ch 7)

Track per video in YouTube Studio:
- **Click-through rate (CTR):** target **6–8%+** (good thumbnails/titles).
- **Average view duration (AVD):** target **~40–50%** of length — the #1 ranking factor.
- **Traffic source:** Browse/Home/Suggested = YouTube is promoting you (good); pure Search early on is fine.

Diagnostic table (Day 8):

| CTR | AVD | Meaning | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| High | Low | Thumbnail over-promises | Make content match the title/thumbnail |
| Low | High | Content's good, packaging weak | Test a new title/thumbnail |
| High | High | It's working | Keep the format |
| Low | Low | Deeper content/delivery issue | Rework topic, pacing, hook |

**Rule:** collect ~20 videos of data before optimizing individual videos. Don't over-react to the first one or two (Day 8, Ch 13).

**Two killers to avoid:**
- **Don't share new videos to social/Facebook groups the day you post** — low-retention traffic tanks organic ranking (Ch 6, Ch 7).
- **Don't run paid ads / "Promote" on your organic videos** — it dilutes AVD/CTR and buries them within days (Ch 9).

## Part 6 — Convert Leads (Day 9, Ch 12)

YouTube leads are pre-sold believers — treat them better than cold leads.

**When they reach out (call/text/DM):**
1. Thank them, then qualify: *"Are you already working with an agent?"*
2. Confirm intent: *"Are you thinking about moving to [market]?"*
3. **Offer a Zoom — don't ask permission.** Give two times: *"I'd love to spend 30–60 min helping you understand the market — Thursday 4pm or Friday 6pm?"*
4. Frame it as **fact-gathering, no pressure** ("so you can make an informed decision, even if you don't move").
5. Get the **spouse/decision-makers** on the call; send the video/info they asked about once the time is set.

**On the Zoom (the filtering system — Ch 12):**
- Ask: why moving, timeline/job start, budget, kids/schools.
- **Bring a local lender** to soft pre-approve live and build confidence.
- Set expectations: *"This market's hot — plan on ~3 offers before you win."*
- Optional **$500 consultation fee + 2-hour tour limit**, refunded at closing, to filter tire-kickers (Ch 11, Ch 12).

**Follow-up cadence (the pipeline — Ch 12):** 6 months out → initial Zoom; 3 months → confirm; final month → weekly. Even "3 years out" leads go on the call — relocations slip and you stay top-of-mind. Happy buyers refer sellers, generating listings for free.

## Part 7 — Scale with Team & Delegation (Ch 16–19)

- **Delegate editing first.** Fiverr editor at **$15–25/video**; expect 3–4 weeks to dial them in, then they outperform you. For thumbnails, shoot 20–30 expression photos against a blank wall and hand them to a $2–5 thumbnail artist (Ch 19).
- **Hire a VA** ($500–700/mo) for calendar, email, uploads, and reminders — it removes the work that breeds resentment and frees you for camera + clients (Ch 19).
- **Template the content:** build ~54 video-title templates per market; agents just "shoot two a week from this list" (Ch 19).
- **Build a closing team so the business isn't trapped in you.** Use a **sliding-scale split** (e.g., 30% deals 1–15 → 40% 16–30 → 50% for life at 31+, self-gen 75/25). A business valued only on *your* production is worth ~nothing when you step away; a team multiplies the valuation (Ch 16).
- **Hire the closer, not just the video person** — leads you generate convert better in a strong closer's hands (Ch 16).
- **Don't over-expand.** ~2 videos/week per channel is sustainable; 20 videos in 2 days is not. Pick markets where *families relocate* (job hubs), not retiree markets that don't scale (Ch 18).

| 14 | Plan delegation: editor, VA, and future closing team |

## Do / Don't Cheat Sheet

**Do:** own your Gmail · name the channel "Living in [Metro]" · use local-only keywords · hook in the first 3–30 sec · publish on a consistent weekly schedule · weave in value-first CTAs · push every lead to a Zoom · bring a lender · delegate editing early · give it 6–12 months.

**Don't:** use a brokerage email · use national "real estate" keywords · perfect the branding for days · read a rigid script · share to social the day you post · run paid ads on organic videos · obsess over the first video's stats · be the only person who can close · chase too many markets at once.
