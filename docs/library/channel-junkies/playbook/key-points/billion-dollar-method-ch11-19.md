## Ch 11 — Be yourself to attract dream clients
- Don't emulate other agents; don't hyper-edit out every pause — it makes you unrelatable.
- Admitting you're new to an area can beat claiming expertise; your "haters" filter for ideal clients.
- Tell stories about *who lives where*, not market facts. Optional $500 consult fee + 2-hour tour limit respects your time.
- Quote [00:26:30]: *"Just tell me what you like, what you don't like, and we can help."*

## Ch 12 — Systems for converting YouTube leads
- YouTube leads are pre-sold believers — the Zoom call is your filter.
- Zoom sequence: why moving → timeline/job start → income (lender soft pre-approves) → kids/schools → set expectation ("~3 offers before you win").
- Bring a **local lender** on the call. Follow-up cadence: 6mo → 3mo → monthly → weekly. Happy buyers refer sellers (free listings).
- Quote [00:14:23]: 20% split until YouTube equals partner's business, then 50/50.

## Ch 13 — Expansion & timeline of growth
- 12–14 months of "pain" before the algorithm works; **consistency beats quality** early.
- One viral keyword unlocks everything (the "pros & cons" video: 3 views → 57k).
- Content pyramid: vlogs → pros/cons → cost of living → map/comparison → schools/lifestyle → (market updates last).
- Titles must answer a search question. CTA added June 4, 2019 = phone calls started.
- Quote [00:08:15]: *"It had three views, nine views... 57,000 views."*

## Ch 14 — Burnout & knowing when to quit
- 12-month rule: no reach-outs by month 12 → pivot or quit. Expensive/large markets take longer.
- Don't let perfectionism stop you (green screen with a baby blanket; shot with face stitches).
- Seattle: quit after 48 videos, sat 10 months, then started working. Depression is data — listen to it.
- Quote [00:08:30]: *"If it doesn't work, let's just stop... I don't waste time and money."*

## Ch 16 — Building team & leaving markets
- A business valued only on *your* production ≈ $0 when you leave; delegating closing multiplies valuation.
- **Sliding-scale split:** deals 1–15 = 30%, 16–30 = 40%, 31–50 = 50% for life; self-gen 75/25.
- Hire the closer first. Heather: 0 → 100 deals year 1, 168 in 18 months. Joe: $50k → $500k.
- Direct, clear leadership beats "just be nice."

## Ch 17 — The "more you give, the less they do" trap
- Don't build a "done-for-you" service if you're the only face — you become customer support and absorb all blame.
- ~97% of course buyers don't follow through; 3% of agency clients succeed — stop trying to save the 97%.
- Pivot to free content + live teaching: walk-and-talk podcast went 400 → 10,000 downloads/month.
- Trader Joe's rule [00:15:15]: keep the top 20%, cut the bottom 80%.

## Ch 18 — The traveling-YouTuber trap
- Multi-market expansion scales revenue but wrecks mental health. Don't expand until your home market is delegated.
- Smaller markets rank faster; choose markets where **families relocate** (job hubs), not retiree markets.
- ~2 videos/week per channel is sustainable; 20 videos in 2 days is not. Stay ~4 weeks ahead.
- Insight [00:22:01]: people searching *many* markets aren't buyers — real relocators have one place in mind.

## Ch 19 — Leverage & delegation
- You won't scale past ~$100k without delegating. Match tasks to your hourly value.
- Roles: admin/calendar VA (~$680/mo), YouTube VA (~$650/mo), content VA ($500–800/mo).
- Editor: Fiverr $15–25/video; 3–4 weeks to dial in, then 10x better than you. Thumbnails: 20–30 expression photos → $2–5 artist.
- Templatize: ~54 title templates per market; agents shoot 2/week from the list.
- Delegation cascade: you (camera/clients/strategy) → VA (admin/uploads) → editor → partner agent (closing) → lender (financing).
- Quote [00:12:34]: *"I needed somebody who could just manage Jackson Wilkie."*

## Master tactics quick reference
- **Lead gen:** authentic relocation vlogs > paid leads; answer the search; simple CTA ("call us if you're moving here").
- **Conversion:** Zoom before tour; lender on call; set "3 offers" expectation; 6mo→weekly follow-up.
- **Production:** consistency > quality early; neighborhood vlogs > market updates; one search question per video; hook immediately.
- **Team:** never be the only closer; sliding-scale split; hire the closer first.
- **Ops:** ~2 videos/week per channel; label clips for the editor; Fiverr editor at $25/video.
- **Mental health:** if you hate a market after ~6 months, quit it.
