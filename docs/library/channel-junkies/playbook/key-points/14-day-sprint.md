# Key Points — 14 Day Sprint (video course)

Per-day extraction from the 14 Day Sprint transcripts. Source: [`../../transcripts/14-day-sprint`](../../transcripts/14-day-sprint).

---

## Day 1 — Create Your Brand Channel
**Goal:** Set up a branded YouTube account as the foundation.
- Create a new Gmail (never a brokerage email — you lose the channel if you leave).
- YouTube Studio → Settings → Channel → Advanced → Create New Channel.
- Name it **"Living in [Metro]"** or **"[City] Team"** — use the largest metro, not a small suburb. Include "Team" even if solo.
- Two-market rule: separate channels only if markets are 8+ hours apart.
- Quote [00:02:47]: *"Once you don't have access to that email anymore, your YouTube channel is done for."*

## Day 2 — Design Your Channel Brand
**Goal:** Professional visuals without perfectionism.
- Use Canva templates: banner (face + city + "new videos every week") and profile photo.
- Cap at ~2 hours. Quote [00:00:56]: *"70% of our students who fail... get stuck on this."*

## Day 3 — Optimize Settings & Enable Features
- Add About description (phone/email/website) + subscribe watermark.
- Verify phone → unlocks custom thumbnails, 15+ min videos, monetization.
- **Keywords: 10–15 LOCAL terms only** ("living in Houston", "Katy homes"). Never "real estate".
- Category = People & Blogs; License = Standard YouTube License.
- Set upload defaults (paste templated description once).
- Quote [00:10:06]: *"Do not create a YouTube channel with your brokerage email."*

## Day 4 — Master Your Video Templates
- Download the "10 Video Banks" (proven titles); start with **"Pros & Cons of Living in [City]" [YEAR]**.
- Read the provided script 3–5x until natural; practice the hook.
- Structure: Hook (0–30s) → Body → CTA throughout. Target 10–15 min (min ~8).
- Hook formula: *"Are you thinking about moving to [City]? Well, [teaser]…"*

## Day 5 — Shoot Your First Video
- Outline bullets on paper (not a full script). One continuous recording; pause/reset on mistakes, wave hand as editor cue.
- Tell stories ("I moved here…"); add personality/humor; re-hook mid-video.
- First videos are imperfect and still work. Quote [00:01:31]: *"I still got this old crappy video... it just sits there and works for me."*

## Day 6 — Edit (iMovie or Filmora)
- Store raw footage on a 1–2 TB external drive; keep computer 50–80% free.
- iMovie (Mac) / Filmora (Windows). **Not Adobe Premiere** ([00:00:11]).
- Cut with Cmd/Ctrl+B; add titles (don't cover face); 5–15s B-roll; CTA text; export with SEO filename.

## Day 7 — Upload (SEO & Metadata)
- Rename file to SEO title first. Paste templated description (keywords + contact + CTA).
- Custom Canva thumbnail (face + emotion + text).
- Made for Kids = NO; Altered/AI content = NO.
- Tags: full title first, then local variations. Geo-tag the city.
- Schedule consistently (e.g., Thursday eve / Saturday morning). Add end screen.
- Quote [00:04:02]: *"The description is very important... we stuff it full of keywords."*

## Day 8 — Analytics
- CTR target 6–8%+ (proven templates 10–15%+). AVD target ~40–50% of length (#1 ranking factor).
- Traffic source: Browse/Home = YouTube promoting you.
- Diagnostics: High CTR + Low AVD = bait/switch; Low CTR + High AVD = weak packaging; both strong = working.
- 20/10 rule: 20 videos in 10 weeks before optimizing individual videos.

## Day 9 — Convert Leads via Consultative Calls
- Acknowledge → qualify ("working with an agent?") → confirm intent → **offer Zoom with two times** (don't ask permission) → frame as fact-gathering → include spouse → send promised info.
- Parasocial: viewers feel they know you. Always push to Zoom; even "3 years out" leads go on the call.
- Quote [00:00:55]: *"YouTube clients... think they know you from your content."*

## Day 10 — Map Videos (highest-performing)
- Record Google Maps via Zoom/Loom; sit in the corner. Hook in first 30s.
- Zoom out → identify hubs → discuss each area (lifestyle/schools/commute) → Street View vlog → tell stories → CTA.
- Results cited: 1,000 subs in 71 days, $13M sold/in-contract from map videos. Highest AVD of any format.
- Quote [00:00:18]: *"You could probably get away with just doing map videos and still kill it."*

## Day 11 — Edit Map Videos (minimal post)
- Import Zoom recording to iMovie; cut dead space; add opening + chapter titles; optional driving B-roll; closing CTA; export with SEO filename.
- Map videos forgive imperfection. Quote [00:00:06]: *"There's no editing involved... people don't care as much on map videos."*

## Day 12 — Upload Map Video (thumbnail & publishing)
- Canva thumbnail: map screenshot + your photo (pointing/excited) + clear text. Send map behind text.
- Publish same as Day 7 (title, templated description, local tags, consistent schedule).

## Day 13 — Monitor Performance & Maintain Consistency
- Track CTR (6–8%+) and AVD (3–4 min min). Don't obsess over the first/second video.
- 20/10 rule; 1–2 videos/week, same day/time. Don't deviate from proven strategy.
- Quote [00:00:10]: *"It is way more imperative that you are getting a lot of videos out."*

## Day 14 — Final Checklist & Commitment
- Recap full setup; next: shoot remaining videos to reach 10 total.
- Critical: stick to the basics — agents who deviate go "from 40 leads a month to zero" ([00:00:37]).

### Tools quick reference
Canva (free) · iMovie (Mac, free) · Filmora (Win, paid) · External HDD 1–2TB · Zoom/Loom · Google Maps · TubeBuddy · dedicated Gmail.
